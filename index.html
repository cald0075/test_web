Welcome to GitHub Pages.

The Computational Hydrodynamics & Biofluids Laboratory team is pleased to announce the public release of the VFS (Virtual Flow Simulator) code. VFS is a state-of-the-art computational fluid mechanics (CFD) software that is capable of simulating complex flows, integrating advanced turbulence models (RANS, LES), a Curvilinear Immersed Boundary (CURVIB) method for handling geometrically complex moving domains, a partitioned fluid-structure interaction (FSI) algorithm, a two-phase free surface flow solver based on the level set method, sediment transport models, etc. These features make VFS a versatile code that is suitable for a broad range of engineering applications, such as in wind and hydrokinetic energy, offshore and nearshore ocean studies, cardiovascular and biological flows, and natural streams and river morphodynamics. The released versions is named VFS-Wind as it contains all the necessary modelling tools for wind energy applications, including land-based [1] and offshore wind farms [2, 3]. Some of the main features of the released version are as follows: • Complex terrain topographies from site-specific locations. • Atmospherics turbulent wind conditions. • In offshore applications, two-phase free surface flows simulations with the presence of realistic complex waves and FSI of complex floating structures moving in 6 degrees of freedom (DOF). • Turbine rotor geometries can be either resolved with the CURVIB method or parameterized with actuator disks or actuator lines. Additional modules, such as the sediment transport, the adaptive mesh refinement (AMR) approach, and the Finite Element methods for flexible structures, will be released in subsequent versions. To illustrate the capabilities of the methods, we show in figure 1 a FSI simulation of a free falling wedge impinging the free surface, resulting complex non-linear phenomena such as breaking waves and structure overtopping. In figure 2, we show a 6 DOF FSI simulation under real ocean wind and wave conditions (see Calderer et al [2,3] for details about this cases). VFS-Wind is the result of more than a decade of development work by several graduate students, post-docs, and research associates under the lead of professor Fotis Sotiropoulos. The novel and advanced CFD modeling techniques, designed from the very beginning to take advantage of massive parallel supercomputers, puts VFS-Wind ahead from most competitor’s products in terms of the high level of fidelity and resolution and the wide range of applicability’s. The code released comes with a detailed user’s manual and a set of case studies designed to facilitate the learning of the various aspects of the code in a comprehensive manner. The included documentation and support material has been elaborated in a collaboration effort with Sandia National Labs. The VFS-Wind source code is distributed under the GNU General Public License (GPL 2.0), offering to the community the flexibility to use and expand the capabilities of the software.
